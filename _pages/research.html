Research Themes

Theme 1: Improving Charge Transfer in Organic Photovoltaic Cells

Among various renewable energy technologies, solar photovoltaics appear as one of the most promising, especially in Africa where the resource is relatively abundant. However, it remains relatively expensive due to dominant silicon technologies. Our research aims to contribute to resolving challenges faced by organic photovoltaic cells, particularly charge transfer at electrode/organic material interfaces.

Theme 2: Study of Photovoltaic Cells Using Wide Band Gap Materials

Recent research in photovoltaics has explored nanostructures like silicon nanowires and wide band gap semiconductors like silicon carbide to improve conversion efficiencies. Our work focuses on developing photovoltaic cells using wide band gap materials such as silicon carbide.

Theme 3: Characterization of Photovoltaic Modules in Local Conditions

Photovoltaic module characteristics are often evaluated under standard test conditions, but actual performance can vary significantly. Our research investigates the real-world performance of photovoltaic modules in local conditions to aid in better system design and optimization.

Optimization of Local Energy Systems

Theme 4: Optimization of Local Cooking Equipment

Efficient cooking equipment is vital for reducing biomass energy consumption. Our research aims to optimize local cooking equipment to minimize fuel usage and environmental impact.

Theme 5: Study of Energy Efficiency Potential and System Optimization

In the context of environmental concerns and increasing energy demands, our work focuses on studying energy efficiency potentials and proposing optimization strategies for local energy systems.
